Steve Ballmer's stealth project

Steve Ballmer was looking for something to do. Connie, his wife, suggested helping with her philanthropic efforts. "But come on," he said. "Doesn’t the government take care of the poor, the sick, the old?" And if not, he wondered, where does all that tax money go? So he and a "small army of economists, professors, and other professionals" created USAFacts, a database that could well be the first nonpartisan effort to create a fully integrated look at revenue and spending across federal, state, and local governments. It was a massive effort, and it's fascinating.
